Their stint dynamics were equally exemplary. ANT demonstrated a remarkable ability to manage tire thermal degradation across all compounds, maintaining a consistent pace throughout their longer initial stint and subsequent shorter bursts. This suggests a finely tuned suspension and brake balance, minimizing stress on the tires and enabling critical lap time consistency. The delta in tire wear compared to front-running rivals was noticeable, indicating a strategic advantage in race pace sustainability.

McLaren's second and third-place finishes for NOR and PIA respectively highlight a significant step forward in their car's overall balance and race performance. PIA, while equally strong, may have pushed his tires marginally harder earlier, resulting in a slightly higher degradation rate towards the end of his second stint, although still well within a podium-contending window.
